Learning Disentangled Representation

 2022/12/24

Recently, I have been trying to understand the 2021 Neurips Spotlight article Self-Supervised Learning Disentangled Group Representation as Feature. While following this line of work, I found this paper particularly interesting. It leverages Invariant Risk Minimization (IRM) and the notion of group theory to shed light on disentangled representation, a research area that has long been investigated. However, as the paper is somehow obsecure to understand, especially for those who do not have backgound in group theory, I would like to share my perspective about this research work based on my understanding. Hopefully, we all can gain new insight from this interesting paper.

1. Introduction

In Self-Supervised Learning Disentangled Group Representation as Feature, the authors aim to achieve two goals: (1) fomulate the notion of “good” representation from a group theoretical view using Higgins’ definition of disentangled representation; (2) propose an iterative self-supervised learning (SSL) algorithm, Interative Parition-based Invariant Risk Minimization (IP-IRM), that can disentangle features beyond augmentation features. The experiment proves that their algorithm converges to a fully disentangled representation.

Although supervised learning of disentangled representation is a well-studied area, unsupervised disentaglement can not be attained beyond augmentation features and is of great significance. If we enable models to learn fully disentangled features from unlabled data, the learned factors of variations are adaptable to different downstream tasks and can futher reduce the cost of labeling and training.
